What is Second Saturday

A worship night that belongs to the whole Church

Second Saturday exists to create a welcoming, joy-filled worship experience where anyone can encounter the love of Jesus and find a place to belong in a local church.

Rooted in the Great Commission, we go where people are, lower every barrier, and make it easy to take a next step toward faith and community. It's one night a month — but the goal is a lifetime of faith and friendship.

“Go therefore and make disciples of all nations…” — Matthew 28:19–20

    What We Believe

    Gathered around the historic essentials

    We gather around the great truths the Church has confessed together across centuries, denominations, and traditions — the shared foundation on which all true Christian fellowship is built.

    The Apostles' Creed

    We believe in God the Father Almighty, maker of heaven and earth. We believe in Jesus Christ, His only Son, our Lord, who was conceived by the Holy Spirit and born of the Virgin Mary, who suffered under Pontius Pilate, was crucified, died, and was buried, who rose again on the third day from the dead, who ascended into heaven and is seated at the right hand of the Father, and who will come again to judge the living and the dead. We believe in the Holy Spirit, the holy universal Church, the communion of saints, the forgiveness of sins, the resurrection of the body, and the life everlasting. Amen.
    • The Bible is the inspired, inerrant, authoritative Word of God and the final rule for faith and life.
    • Salvation is by grace alone, through faith alone, in Christ alone.
    • Every believer is called to life in community with the local church.
    • Jesus is Lord over all: past, present, and future.
    A note on our unity

    We recognize that faithful, Bible-believing churches hold a range of convictions on secondary matters — modes of baptism, worship style, church governance, spiritual gifts, and more. Second Saturday is not the place to adjudicate those differences; we celebrate them as evidence of a rich and diverse body of Christ. When questions arise, we point people back to Scripture and their home church. Our unity rests not on uniformity, but on the person and work of Jesus Christ, in whom all things hold together (Colossians 1:17).
